Optics — Total Internal Reflection

Fibre Optic Cable

Light launched into a glass fibre bounces off the inner walls. When the angle of incidence exceeds the critical angle θc = arcsin(1/n), the wall is a perfect mirror — that's total internal reflection. Bend the fibre too sharply and rays hit the wall too steeply; they leak out instead. Drag the handles to reshape the cable, and try changing n and the wavelength (a touch of dispersion is baked in: n drifts slightly with λ).
