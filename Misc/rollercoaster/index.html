About this simulation

Build a coaster piece by piece, send a train around it, and watch the energy move. Every metre the train climbs is gravitational potential energy bought with kinetic energy — Ep = mgh and Ek = ½mv² trading back and forth along the track. The chain lift is the only thing adding energy to the ride.

Scale. One tile is 4 m square and one height step is 1 m, so a lift hill of 10 steps is a 10 m climb. Heights are measured from ground level.

Energy. The Energy window shows the train's kinetic and gravitational potential energy as the ride runs; Graph plots them against position along the track, and Report gives the ride's statistics once the train is back at the station.

View. Drag to pan, scroll to zoom, ⟲ ⟳ to rotate the park.

Building. Track grows from the yellow arrow. Pick a direction and the slope you want to be travelling at, then press Build this — the transition onto that slope is added for you, so holding a climb just means pressing build again. Anything the track can't do from where it currently is goes grey. Backspace removes the last piece.

Banking. Turns can be built flat or banked at 45°. Run the same corner both ways at the same speed, and watch the lateral g in the ride report.
