About this simulation

Build a coaster piece by piece, send a train around it, and watch the energy move. Every metre the train climbs is gravitational potential energy bought with kinetic energy — Ep = mgh and Ek = ½mv² trading back and forth along the track. The chain lift is the only thing adding energy to the ride.

Scale. One tile is 4 m square and one height step is 1 m, so a lift hill of 10 steps is a 10 m climb. Heights are measured from ground level.

View. Drag to pan, scroll to zoom, ⟲ ⟳ to rotate the park.

Building. Pieces attach to the yellow arrow, which is the end of the track so far. A piece is only offered if its entry matches the slope the track is currently on, so a steep drop has to be levelled out before it can turn. Backspace removes the last piece.
