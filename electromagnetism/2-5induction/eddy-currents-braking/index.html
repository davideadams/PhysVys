Eddy Currents & Magnetic Braking

Drop a conducting plate through a magnet's gap and the changing flux induces swirling eddy currents. By Lenz's law those currents feel a force that opposes the motion — the plate brakes. Compare two plates side-by-side, slot one to break the current loops, or sweep a coin over a metal-detector coil and watch the signal spike.

How it works
The gate's transmit coil makes an oscillating B field across the walkway. As metal moves through, the changing flux induces eddy currents in it (Faraday). Those currents make their own field, opposing the change (Lenz) — and that reaction is what the receive coil reads as a signal. Drag the person, or hit Auto-walk.
