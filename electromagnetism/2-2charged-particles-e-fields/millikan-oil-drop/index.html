SACE Stage 2 Physics — Topic 2: Electricity and Magnetism

Millikan's Oil Drop

Charged oil drops drift between two parallel plates. When the electric force on a drop exactly balances gravity, it hangs perfectly still. Measure its radius with the microscope, balance the forces, and see what you can learn about the charge on each drop.

How to use

Click Spritz to puff oil drops into the chamber from the atomiser. Most will rise or fall — but a few will balance and hang motionless. It often takes several spritzes to collect enough stable drops.

You can adjust the plate voltage V. Changing it shifts which drops happen to balance: make sure you record the voltage you used alongside each measurement.

Click any drop (moving or still) to examine it under the microscope. Read its radius from the scale.

For a stationary drop the electric force balances gravity: qE = mg. Use the constants above together with your measured radius to calculate the drop's charge q. Collect a handful of drops in a spreadsheet and look carefully at the numbers you get.

No drag or buoyancy is modelled. In reality a drop reaches terminal velocity almost immediately, so a "floating" drop really is in true force balance.
