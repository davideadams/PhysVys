SACE Stage 2 Physics — Electricity & Magnetism 2.4

Magnetism from Relativity

A current-carrying wire is electrically neutral in the lab — yet a test charge moving alongside it feels a force. Hop into the test charge's frame: the moving ion lattice contracts, the now-stationary electrons spread out, and the wire picks up a net charge. The magnetic force in the lab is just an electric force in disguise. Drift speeds here are exaggerated so length contraction is visible.
