SACE Stage 2 Physics — Motion 1.4

Relativistic Momentum

Classical physics predicts momentum grows linearly with speed. Relativity says otherwise — and the difference explains why nothing with mass can ever reach \(c\).

Controls

Why can't we reach \(c\)?

As \(v \to c\), the Lorentz factor \(\gamma \to \infty\), so relativistic momentum \(p = \gamma m_0 v\) diverges. A constant force keeps adding momentum at a steady rate — but each unit of extra momentum produces less and less extra speed. Reaching \(v = c\) would require infinite momentum, and therefore infinite energy.
