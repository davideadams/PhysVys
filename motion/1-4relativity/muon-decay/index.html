Phase 3 — Classical Survival Prediction

For each muon detected at Mt Washington, we drew a line starting at 1910 m and extending downward by the distance that muon should travel before decay, assuming it moves at 0.994 c. Count how many lines reach sea level.

How is distance calculated?

All muons travel at approximately 0.994 c. Once we know the time t between detection and decay, the distance travelled before decay is simply

d = 0.994 × 3 × 108 m/s × t

A muon that decays after 2.2 μs (the mean lifetime) travels only about 660 m — nowhere near the 1910 m needed to reach sea level from Mt Washington. A muon would need to live for ≈ 6.38 μs to make it all the way down, which is almost three mean lifetimes.

Count how many of your Phase 1 muons reach sea level according to this classical picture. Compare that number to the count measured at sea level in Phase 2.
