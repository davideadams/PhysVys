Mode 1: Geostationary transfer (beginner)

Start in a low equatorial orbit. Transfer to the white geostationary ring and circularize. A red ground marker rotates with Earth — a true geostationary satellite appears stationary above it.

Tips

  • Don’t point at the target and thrust! Instead, thrust prograde (along your direction of travel) to raise the far side of your orbit.
  • This is a Hohmann transfer: burn at periapsis to raise apoapsis, coast to apoapsis, then burn again to circularize.
  • The dashed white line shows your predicted orbit — use it to aim!
  • Lower Δv = better score.

The physics

Gravity follows an inverse-square law: F = GMm/r². Orbital speed at radius r is v = √(GM/r) — higher orbits are slower. A geostationary orbit is a circular orbit whose period equals Earth’s rotation period, so the satellite hovers over a fixed ground point.
