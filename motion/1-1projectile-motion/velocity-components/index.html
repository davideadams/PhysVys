SACE Stage 2 Physics — Motion 1.1

Independence of Velocity Components

A ball dropped from rest and a ball launched horizontally from the same height fall together — reaching the ground at exactly the same time. Gravity acts identically on both; only horizontal motion differs.

Key idea

Vertical motion depends only on gravity — not on horizontal velocity. Both balls experience the same downward acceleration g, so at every instant they share the same height. The launched ball's horizontal motion is completely independent: it travels at constant vh while falling, unaffected by gravity.
