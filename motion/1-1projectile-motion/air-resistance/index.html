Effect of Air Resistance

Compare how air resistance changes range, maximum height and time of flight. Select a projectile preset or adjust the drag coefficient to explore the effect.

Key idea

Air resistance (drag) opposes motion and acts along the velocity vector, reducing both horizontal and vertical speed. The drag coefficient β = CdAρ/2m captures how strongly a projectile is affected relative to its mass. A heavy shot put barely notices the air; a shuttlecock is dominated by it.
