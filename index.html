SACE Stage 2 Physics

Interactive Simulation Menu

A growing set of classroom-ready HTML models for Stage 2 Physics, organised by topic and subtopic so students and teachers can jump straight to the right idea.

Topic 1

Motion and Relativity

1.1 Projectile Motion

Trajectory ExplorerAdjust launch speed, angle and height to shape a projectile's flight path. Independence of Velocity ComponentsCompare the horizontal and vertical motion of a projectile side by side. Effect of Air ResistanceAdd drag to a projectile and see how the trajectory changes. Terminal VelocityDrop an object through air and watch speed settle as drag grows. GorillasClassic artillery game — set angle and power to land a banana on target.

1.2 Forces and Momentum

Impulse & ΔpApply a force for a chosen time and track the change in momentum. 2D CollisionsSet up two pucks colliding at an angle and check momentum conservation. 2D ExplosionsSplit a stationary object into fragments and analyse the resulting momenta. PropulsionEject mass from a craft and observe the recoil that drives it forward. CrokinoleFlick discs on a board — a playful sandbox for 2D elastic collisions.

1.3 Circular Motion and Gravitation

Uniform Circular MotionSpin an object in a circle and inspect its velocity and centripetal force. Banked CurvesAdjust bank angle, speed and friction for a car rounding a curve. Newtonian GravitationVary masses and separation to explore the inverse-square gravitational force. SatellitesLaunch satellites at different altitudes and speeds to see the orbits that result. Kepler's LawsExplore orbital shape, period and swept areas for planets around a star.

1.4 Relativity

Galilean RelativitySwitch between reference frames to see how classical velocities add. Relativity of SimultaneityTrigger events from different frames and compare when each observer sees them. Length Contraction & Time DilationDial up the speed of a moving object and watch its clocks and rulers. Relativistic MomentumGraph momentum against speed as particles approach the speed of light. Muon Decay ExperimentCount cosmic-ray muons arriving at sea level and compare to classical predictions.

Topic 2

Electricity and Magnetism

2.1 Electric Fields

Coulomb's Law ExplorerVary charge and separation to measure the force between two point charges. Superposition of ForcesPlace multiple charges and read off the net force on a chosen test charge. Electric Field VisualiserArrange charges and see the resulting field lines and vector map. Field Strength vs DistancePlot how E falls off with distance from a point charge or charged plate. Hollow ConductorsProbe the field inside and outside charged conductors of different shapes.

2.2 Charged Particles in Electric Fields

Particle DeflectionFire charged particles between parallel plates and steer them with the field. Millikan's Oil DropBalance tiny charged droplets between gravity and an adjustable electric field.

2.3 Magnetic Fields

Straight Conductor & Right-Hand RuleOrbit a 3D wire to see the circular field loops and probe the field at any point. Dipoles & SolenoidsCompare the field around a bar magnet with that of a current-carrying solenoid.

2.4 Charged Particles in Magnetic Fields

Force on a Current-Carrying ConductorTilt a wire in a uniform field and watch F = BIℓ sin θ vanish at parallel. Two Parallel WiresSuperpose the fields of two long wires; parallel currents attract, antiparallel repel. Charged Particle in a Uniform B FieldFire a charge into a uniform field and watch it loop with radius r = mv / |q|B. CyclotronTune the oscillator to f = qB/2πm and accelerate the particle to the dee rim. Magnetism from RelativitySwitch frames to see the magnetic force on a charge become an electric force from length contraction.
